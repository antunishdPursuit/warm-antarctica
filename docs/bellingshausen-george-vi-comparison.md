# Bellingshausen Sea and George VI Ice Shelf comparison

## Purpose

This comparison lets a visitor switch the local warm-water view from the Amundsen Sea to George VI Ice Shelf in the eastern Bellingshausen Sea. It does not add a new global journey or imply that water from either shelf travels to New York City.

## Supported claim

The British Antarctic Survey reports that Circumpolar Deep Water, warmer than 1 degree C in the study, flooded the eastern Bellingshausen continental shelf and formed the main inflow beneath George VI Ice Shelf.

- [British Antarctic Survey: Circulation and melting beneath George VI Ice Shelf](https://www.bas.ac.uk/data/our-data/publication/circulation-and-melting-beneath-george-vi-ice-shelf-antarctica-2/)

The map text stays narrower than the research result: it says this can happen in the eastern Bellingshausen Sea and does not make a claim about the full sea or all nearby ice shelves.

## Visual rules

- The orange paths and particles are guided illustrations, not measured parcel tracks or live current data.
- They show only at the Warm water and Antarctica stops.
- The local heat halo moves with the selected shelf.
- The existing Global ocean and New York cards remain unchanged.
- Reduced-motion visitors see the static paths without moving particles.

## Verification

1. Select **George VI** in the comparison controls.
2. Confirm the map moves to the eastern Bellingshausen Sea and the first card uses the BAS source.
3. Select **Antarctica** and confirm the heat halo moves to the George VI local view.
4. Select **Global ocean** and **New York** and confirm no local shelf particles remain.
5. Switch back to **Amundsen** and confirm the NASA Pine Island source and Amundsen path return.
