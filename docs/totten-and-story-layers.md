# Totten Glacier and story layers

## Totten comparison

The third shelf choice moves the local warm-water view to Totten Glacier on the Sabrina Coast of East Antarctica.

NASA reports that researchers found seafloor troughs deep enough to give warm ocean water access to Totten Glacier's base. The map therefore shows a local explanatory path from deeper offshore water through the troughs toward the ice shelf.

- [NASA JPL: UTexas-NASA Study Sees New Threat to East Antarctic Ice](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/utexas-nasa-study-sees-new-threat-to-east-antarctic-ice/)

The paths and particles do not show live current data, a measured parcel track, or a route to New York City.

## Story layers

The Layers control can show or hide three existing teaching visuals:

- **Warm-water paths:** the local, selected shelf's static paths and guided particles.
- **Ice-shelf response:** the orange local heat halo at the Antarctica stop.
- **Global connection:** the global-ocean bands and symbolic New York water-level cue.

The controls do not alter the source-backed card text. They give learners a way to isolate one part of the causal story.
