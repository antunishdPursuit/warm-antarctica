# Guided Amundsen particles

## Purpose

The moving orange points show the existing, source-backed Amundsen Sea learning path more clearly. They are a guided illustration of relatively warm water reaching toward an ice shelf through deep channels.

They do **not** show live ocean-current data, a forecast, water moving to New York City, or a measured track for a single parcel of water.

## Evidence and scope

The learning card links to NASA's [Channel Beneath Pine Island Glacier](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/channel-beneath-pine-island-glacier-48637/). NASA describes the role of channels and ocean water near Pine Island Glacier. The particle paths remain local to the Amundsen Sea and end at the ice-shelf heat point.

## Motion and access

- The particles move only at the Warm water and Antarctica stops.
- They stop when the visitor uses a reduced-motion setting.
- The static paths remain visible when motion is reduced.
- The card's existing beneath-the-shelf cutaway explains the layer below the floating shelf.

## Verification

1. Select **Warm water** and confirm two particles move along each orange path.
2. Select **Antarctica** and confirm the particles remain local while the cutaway appears.
3. Select **Global ocean** or **New York** and confirm the particles disappear.
4. Enable reduced motion and confirm the paths stay visible but the points do not move.
